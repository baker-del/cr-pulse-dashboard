Key Takeaways
FindingDetailRecommendation
SF is the standout 17 registrants → $28K closed + $140.7K pipeline. 5 deals (29% deal rate). Best pipeline density of any bootcamp by far. Double down on West Coast AEC. Consider follow-up SF event in H2 2026.
Columbia MD generated most revenue ~15 registrants → $40K closed won (Big-D). However, $123.9K in closed-lost shows deals were created but didn't convert. Strong market for generating pipeline. Review lost deal reasons (HITT $74K, Davis $50K) — was it timing, product fit, or pricing?
Columbia SC: high attendance, zero pipeline $ Largest cohort (~25) but $0 in active or won pipeline. 3 deals all at $0 or lost. Investigate: Were BDRs actively working these? Are SC firms the right ICP? May need different nurture for smaller SE firms.
NYC: zero deals created ~10 registrants, all MQL, no SQL progression, no deals at all. Either wrong ICP or follow-up gap. Re-engage with targeted outreach or different value prop for NYC AEC market.
LA showing early promise 11 registrants, 3 SQLs already, $36K pipeline. Highest SQL conversion rate (27%). Still early (6 weeks old). Monitor closely — LA could match SF trajectory.
Program ROI is strong at 81% $68K won on $37.5K spend. With $187K pipeline, potential ROI is 580%+. $409K total pipeline generated. CX Bootcamps are a proven channel. Scale to 8-10/year if unit economics hold.
CRM hygiene needs work 25% of bootcamp deals missing deal_source tag. No "which bootcamp" field on contacts. 1) Add "Bootcamp Attended" multi-select property. 2) Automate deal_source from contact lead_source via workflow.
